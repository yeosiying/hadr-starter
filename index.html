What is this

A personal early-warning page for humanitarian disasters.

Three feeds — GDACS, USGS, ReliefWeb — each report disasters differently, on their own schedule, and none of them push. HADR Monitor polls all three, archives every payload, and folds the overlap so one earthquake shows up once — with each source's claim underneath. You get current alerts, recent changes, and a health banner on a single local page. Nothing to log into.

Why it works this way — and where it's headed

Magnitude is not the same thing as harm.

A raw seismic feed reports physics. It can't tell these two apart — HADR Monitor can:

M7.5 · empty ocean
Huge on the Richter scale. No one near it.
▪ stays silent
M6.2 · under a city
Smaller quake. Millions exposed.
▲ alerts you

It triggers on modeled human impact — “this will affect people” — not on “the ground moved.”

Trustworthy silence is the other rule: a stalled feed turns the banner red instead of vanishing into calm. No alerts and no banner = genuinely nothing happening.

Where it's headed: a foundation built to grow — ReliefWeb enrichment switches on when its access is approved, the full payload archive makes any alert replayable, and thresholds are config, not code, meant to be tuned against real traffic.

How the pieces fit

Three feeds, read as layers — not rivals.

📈 USGS — did the ground move? Fast seismic detection; physical severity only. Records get revised, re-keyed, deleted. minutes
🌍 GDACS — will it hurt people? Modeled exposure × vulnerability. Green / Orange / Red decides whether you're alerted. ~25 min
📰 ReliefWeb — what do humans say? Editorial confirmation + the GLIDE join key. Enriches an event — never triggers one. hours–days

Who it's for

One person who needs to notice quickly.

The reader

A single HADR-interested owner. No team, no accounts. They open a page on their own machine; a snapshot is written each morning at 08:30.

The decision it speeds up

“Is anything happening right now I should pay attention to?” One glance at a pre-filtered, de-duplicated page — not a three-site morning ritual.

What it deliberately does not do

The scope it refuses — on purpose.

Each is a decision. Staying small is how the part that matters stays trustworthy.

  • No push. A page you pull, on localhost.
  • Not multi-user. One owner, no auth or teams.
  • No severity ranking. Attention isn't importance.
  • No forecasting. Reports what feeds assert.
  • Quiet hazards. Volcanoes/droughts stored, not alerted; wildfires only at Red.
  • ReliefWeb never triggers. It only confirms.
  • No pruning. Everything kept as the replay corpus.
  • Nothing public. No hosting, nothing to secure.
